# Privacy Policy — Flying Pig

_Last updated: 23 July 2026_

This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile game **Flying Pig** ("the App", "we", "us")
handles information. By using the App you agree to this policy.

> **Publishing note (not part of the policy):** Google Play requires a privacy policy
> reachable at a public URL. Host this text (e.g. GitHub Pages, a Google Site, or any
> web page) and paste that URL into the Play Console listing and App content →
> Privacy policy. Update the "Last updated" date and the contact email if it changes.

## Summary

- Flying Pig does **not** ask for your real name, email, phone number, or an account
  login. We never collect information that identifies you personally.
- Your local high scores are stored **only on your device** and are never sent to us.
- If you play with the **live global rankings** feature, your chosen nickname, a random
  device identifier, your score, and your platform (Android/iOS) are sent to our
  database provider (Supabase) so a global leaderboard can be shown. None of this is
  tied to your real identity.
- The App shows ads through **Google AdMob**, which may collect certain data (such as a
  device advertising identifier) to serve and measure ads. This is controlled by Google.

## Information we collect directly

**None that identifies you.** The App keeps your best score and local leaderboard
(including any name you type into the local high-score list) in on-device storage
(`localStorage`). This data never leaves your device and is removed if you clear the
App's data or uninstall it.

## Live global rankings (Supabase)

Flying Pig optionally shows a live global leaderboard alongside your local high scores.
If this feature is active, the App sends the following to our database provider
(Supabase) whenever you complete a qualifying run:

- a nickname/player name you choose (or a default one),
- an optional account username you set,
- a random identifier generated and stored on your device (not your name, email, or any
  account — just a random value used to recognize repeat submissions from the same
  device),
- your score, and
- your platform (Android or iOS).

This lets the App show a global ranking and your position on it. It is not linked to
your real identity, and no advertising or tracking identifier is used for this feature.
This data is retained by our database provider until we remove it; contact us (below) if
you'd like your entry removed.

## Information collected by third parties (advertising)

The App uses **Google AdMob** to display banner advertising. Google may collect and
process data as described in its own policies, which can include:

- a device/advertising identifier,
- approximate, non-precise location inferred from IP address,
- device and diagnostic information,
- ad interaction and performance data.

This processing is governed by Google's policies, not ours:

- How Google uses information from sites/apps that use its services:
  https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites
- Google Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

You can limit ad personalization on your device via **Settings → Google → Ads**
(reset or delete your advertising ID / opt out of personalized ads).

## Children

Flying Pig is family-friendly and contains no adult content. It is not directed to
children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
Advertising is served in a standard (not child-directed) configuration; if the App is
ever targeted to children, ad settings must be updated to comply with Google Play's
Families policy and applicable law (COPPA/GDPR-K).

## Data security & retention

We hold no data that identifies you personally. On-device data persists until you clear
App data or uninstall. Live-ranking data (nickname, device identifier, score, platform)
is retained by our database provider (Supabase) until removed. Third-party ad data is
retained per Google's policies.

## Your rights

Depending on your region (e.g. GDPR/CCPA), you may have rights over data processed by
advertising partners. Exercise these through Google's controls linked above and your
device's ad settings.
